# Naming — working title "Showup"

Voice-first AI goal-coach that calls you, asks if you did the thing, and rescues momentum without guilt.

## 1. Candidates

| Name | Evokes | Why it fits | Domain hunch | Verb (1-5) | Warmth (1-5) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Nudge** | A gentle elbow from a friend | Captures the call's tone: small, kind, persistent | Likely taken (.com) | 5 — "Nudge me at 7" | 4 |
| **Ringback** | A returned call, a resonance | Literal call mechanic + emotional callback to your goal | Available-ish | 4 — "Ringback my run" | 4 |
| **Onward** | Forward motion after a stumble | The rough-day / hit-a-wall ethos in one word | Specialty TLD only | 3 | 5 |
| **Beckon** | A warm summon, not a buzz | Voice-coach calling you toward the goal | Available | 4 — "Beckon me at dawn" | 5 |
| **Kindly** | Politeness + warmth, slight wink | Tonal opposite of hustle apps | Likely taken | 3 | 5 |
| **Holler** | A friend yelling across the yard | Calls feel casual, not corporate | Specialty TLD only | 5 — "Holler at me 7am" | 4 |
| **Tether** | A line back to your intention | Recovery + connection, not punishment | Available | 3 | 4 |
| **Pip** | A short tone, a small seed | Tiny, warm, memorable; the ring before the call | Likely taken (.com) | 4 — "Pip me tomorrow" | 5 |
| **Carryon** | Persistence + lightness | "Keep going" energy without grit-speak | Specialty TLD only | 4 — "Carryon'd my week" | 5 |
| **Echo** | Voice + a returned signal | The AI calls; the goal echoes back to you | Cooked (Amazon) | 4 | 4 |

## 2. Top 3

**Beckon.** Wins on tone. A beckon is a hand-wave, not an alarm — exactly what differentiates this from streak-shaming apps. Works as a verb in copy ("Beckon me Tuesday at 6"), pairs naturally with the framework choice ("Atomic Beckon"), and the domain space is unusually quiet for a real English word. The slightly old-fashioned register reads as human in a category drowning in "AI" suffixes.

**Ringback.** Most literal fit for the product mechanic — it *is* a call coming back to you. Doubles as emotional metaphor: your past self rings your present self. Verbable, ownable, and lives outside the cooked productivity lexicon. Risk: telecom associations may need a logo that leans warm to override.

**Onward.** The strongest emotional fit for the rough-day / hit-a-wall thesis. "Onward" is what a kind friend says when you fall short — never punitive, always forward. Weaker as a verb, but a great brand word and tagline engine ("Onward, not over"). Likely needs a creative TLD (onward.app, getonward.com) but the word itself is uncrowded in the AI-coach space.

## 3. Do not pick

- **Echo** — Amazon owns the mental real estate; voice-product collision is fatal.
- **Calm / Headspace-adjacent** (e.g. "Stillwell", "Hush") — wellness category is saturated and the app isn't meditation; positioning drift.
- **Streakly / Habitr / Focusly** — uses the exact words the brief bans; also dates the product to 2018-era app naming.
- **Noom-style coined words** (e.g. "Yumo", "Velo") — high legal risk of collision with existing fitness/scooter brands, and coined names need ad budgets the app likely doesn't have at MVP.
- **Anything ending in ".ai"** as the name itself (e.g. "Coach.ai", "Goal.ai") — corporate, cold, violates the warmth constraint, and signals commodity wrapper rather than product.
